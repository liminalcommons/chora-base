## What We're Building

Liminal Commons is developing **coordination infrastructure for community resilience**, inspired by the Acropolis OS vision of symbiotic intelligence - humans and AI working together to support thriving communities.

### The Vision: Acropolis OS

From our research on community coordination systems:

> "We envision a world populated by resilient, self-aware, and adaptable communities. These communities function like thriving ecosystems, capable of deep self-understanding, effective self-governance, enhanced adaptability, and harnessing symbiotic intelligence."

**Core components**:
- **Ontology**: Shared conceptual maps for community understanding
- **Knowledge Base**: Collective memory (knowledge graphs + vector databases)
- **Community AI Agent**: Context-aware assistance for coordination

**Key principle**: "Symbiotic Collaboration - Centering the human element, using AI to augment collective capabilities, not replace human judgment or connection."

### Our Work: Testing at Small Scale

chora-workspace and the Liminal Commons ecosystem serve as a **proof-of-concept** for coordination patterns that could scale to community infrastructure:

```
Acropolis OS (Vision)
  → Symbiotic intelligence infrastructure for communities

chora-workspace (Implementation)
  → Testing coordination patterns at ecosystem scale (3-5 repos)

chora-base (Toolkit)
  → Reusable components for building such systems
```

**What we're testing**:
- How do repositories coordinate without hierarchical control?
- How do we preserve autonomy while enabling collaboration?
- How do AI agents participate as peers, not servants?
- How do we accumulate knowledge across coordination cycles?
- How do we measure effectiveness without extractive metrics?

## Why We Share Coordination Patterns

### Commons Orientation

From our Liminal Grants business model:

**Not**: Extract value from projects we support
**Instead**: Create network effects where each success makes the next easier

**Structure**:
- 90% of resources flow to projects
- 10% covers operations AND ecosystem development
- Success-based alignment (mutual benefit, not extraction)

**Applied to coordination**:
- Patterns we discover benefit all repos
- Templates evolve through shared use
- Knowledge accumulates for everyone
- Network effects compound value

### Bounded Rationality & Humility

We acknowledge:
- **We can't know everything** - our context is one among many
- **Patterns are context-dependent** - what worked for us may not work for you
- **Evidence is limited** - 4 sprints in one ecosystem is a small sample
- **We're learning as we go** - these patterns are evolving, not finished

Therefore:
- We **share what we observed**, not prescribe what you should do
- We **offer templates freely**, not sell solutions
- We **invite discernment**, not deference to our approach
- We **document humbly**, not claim universal truth

### Provide Value Freely

**Wherever we can, to whoever we can, in whatever way we can.**

This means:
- Transparent sharing of learnings (knowledge notes, ROI reports, metrics)
- Open templates (capability declarations, coordination requests)
- Documented patterns (what worked, what didn't, why)
- Git-native infrastructure (no proprietary lock-in)

**But also**:
- Respect for different contexts (your needs may differ)
- Invitation to evolution (take what serves, change what doesn't)
- Acknowledgment of our limits (we don't have all answers)

## The "Gameworld" - Living Coordination Ecosystem

When we talk about building a "gameworld," we mean:

**Not**: A literal game (TTRPG, video game, simulation)

**Instead**: The **coordination ecosystem itself** as a living, evolving system where:
- Projects coordinate through shared protocols (inbox, SAPs)
- Knowledge accumulates through feedback loops (Memory → Metrics → Awareness)
- Patterns emerge from practice (not prescribed top-down)
- AI and humans collaborate as peers (not tools and users)
- Value compounds through network effects (each success enables others)

**The "game"** is the meta-challenge of:
- Discovering what enables effective coordination
- Evolving patterns that serve diverse contexts
- Building infrastructure that supports community resilience
- Creating generative relationships (where collaboration strengthens both parties)
